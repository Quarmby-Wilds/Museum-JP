Hidden Histories of Japan

Welcome to the Hidden Histories of Japan Museum

Unearthing the stories beneath the surface.

Across Japan's islands lie traces of lives that shaped — and reshaped — the nation's identity. The Hidden Histories of Japan Museum explores the archaeology and anthropology of communities whose stories have too often been buried: the Ainu of the north, the Ryukyuans of the south, and generations of farmers, fishers, artisans, and believers who left their mark in clay, stone, and memory. Step inside a museum where the past is not silent — it speaks through the hands that built, the voices that endured, and the earth that remembers.

Featured Experiences:

  • Walk through reconstructed Jōmon pit dwellings
  • Hear Ainu oral traditions told through immersive sound
  • Explore modern rediscoveries of lost villages and hidden faiths
